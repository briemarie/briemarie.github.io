As a part of DevBootcamp's curriculum, I completed a solo app challenge during phase 2, week 6. I developed an app, Artsee, which consumes the Brooklyn Museum API to allow for quick art searches based on a desired artist. A future feature of the app will be a music player that cross references the chosen artwork's creation date and plays music that could have possibly inspired the artist while at work on the piece.

In order to graduate DevBootcamp, groups completed larger scale apps. My team's app, Vendable, was developed using a Node back-end and an Angular/Ionic front-end mobile iOS design. The app is meant to simplify the grocery shopping experience in a big city- complete with a MongoDB of over 100 thousand items found at big chain grocery stores, the app allows a user to find stores based on a 5 mile radius and create a shopping list of desired items. The list pulls the data and updates the user's list with item prices and a grand total. When the user is ready, the app uses the Google Maps API to route the directions to the chosen store.
